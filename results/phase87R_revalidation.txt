ISSUE 3: NULL MODEL 1 (ROW-PERMUTED) IS INVALID
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Row permutation of T_raw preserves individual row sparsity patterns
  but destroys the correspondence between row identity and transitions.
  Random stochastic matrices with similar sparsity have gaps ~0.73.
  This means row-permuted null tests ROW SPARSITY, not linguistic structure.

  Random 50×50 stochastic (48% density): gap = 0.735 ± 0.018
  Row-permuted VMS null:                  gap = 0.723 ± 0.140
  VMS actual:                              gap = 0.470

  All three are in the same range. The row-permuted null does NOT
  test for linguistic structure. NULL MODEL 1 IS RETRACTED.

  Null model 2 (within-word shuffle, gap=0.289, z=68) IS valid
  and confirms that chunk ordering within words is STRUCTURED.

PHASE 87R — REVALIDATION SYNTHESIS
============================================================================

  BUGS FOUND IN PHASE 87:
  1. Purity metric was recall-only (masked 42% precision in Latin)
  2. Null model 1 (row-permuted) tested sparsity, not structure → RETRACTED
  3. Stability check had sign-flip bug (fixed: actual stability 96-100%)

  METHODOLOGICAL PROBLEMS:
  1. Sign(0) spectral V/C gives mean F1 = 0.48 across NL baselines
     (Latin 0.56, Italian 0.62, English 0.24, French 0.33)
     This is too weak to draw conclusions from VMS data
  2. 59% of VMS chunk bigrams are I→F from 2-chunk words (vs 1-8% in NL)
     Positional structure NECESSARILY dominates the bigram matrix
  3. Syllable control failed (syllable gaps ≈ character gaps)
  4. NL λ2 sign is not universally negative (English is positive)

  GENUINE FINDINGS THAT SURVIVE:
  1. VMS chunk ordering is SIGNIFICANTLY structured (z=68 vs shuffle null)
  2. Chunk spectral gap (0.470) is in the NL character range (z=-1.13)
  3. Chunk λ2 is NEGATIVE (-0.53), matching 3/4 NL languages
     → binary alternation structure EXISTS in chunk sequences
  4. Glyph spectral gap (0.975) is FAR outside NL range (z=+5.7)
     → glyph transitions are near-random (slot grammar dominates)
     → confirms glyphs are sub-character, chunks are character-like
  5. The chunk binary split is STABLE (96-100% across matrix sizes)
     and CROSS-DIALECTAL (Jaccard 0.615 between Currier A and B)
  6. The split IS positional (I vs F) — but this is NOT disqualifying:
     NL characters ALSO have strong positional preferences
     (capital letters, word-final patterns, etc.)

  REVISED INTERPRETATION:
  The spectral V/C test was the WRONG TEST for VMS chunks because:
  a) Most VMS words have only 2 chunks → one bigram → pure I/F structure
  b) The spectral method itself is weak (F1 = 0.24-0.62 on NL)
  c) Positional and V/C effects are CONFOUNDED and cannot be separated
     with 2-chunk-dominant data

  However, the EIGENVALUE STRUCTURE comparison is informative:
  - VMS chunks behave like NL characters (gap in range, negative λ2)
  - VMS glyphs do NOT (gap 0.975, near-zero λ2)
  This independently confirms Phase 85/86: chunks are the right unit.

  AMENDED VERDICT:
  Phase 87 result: INCONCLUSIVE (confirmed — not upgraded or downgraded)
  Phase 87R adds:  Eigenvalue structure comparison supports chunks as
                   character-level units (gap and λ2 sign match NL).
                   The spectral V/C test itself is too weak to use.
