STEP 9: CRITICAL SYNTHESIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  FINDING 1 — CORPUS SIZE PROBLEM (FATAL FOR FULL FINGERPRINT):
    The OHG/MHG charm corpus (587 tokens) is ~80× smaller
    than VMS (40,368 tokens). Full fingerprint distance
    comparison is STATISTICALLY MEANINGLESS at this scale.
    → Heaps β, Zipf α, word-bigram entropy are UNRELIABLE.
    → Only word-length and character-bigram metrics are usable.

  FINDING 2 — WORD LENGTH (THE ROBUST METRIC):
    Charm mean word length: 4.67
    VMS mean word length:   5.10
    BvgS mean word length:  4.19
    Faust mean word length: 4.78

  FINDING 3 — h_char ANOMALY PERSISTENCE:
    Charm h_char: 0.8424  (VMS target: 0.641)
    The h_char anomaly persists: no natural language plaintext
    (including charms) matches VMS's 0.641.

  FINDING 4 — STRUCTURAL PROPERTIES OF CHARMS:
    Charms show: repetitive formulaic structure ('so' appears 9×)
    + varied lexical items (body parts, divine names, plants)
    + bilingual German/Latin mixing (4% Latin)
    + short formulaic words (mean 4.7 chars)
    These properties PARTIALLY match the VMS's combination of
    formulaic structure + lexical variety. But they do NOT explain
    the h_char anomaly, which requires an encoding layer.

  FINDING 5 — THE REAL LESSON FROM PHASE 81+82:
    Phase 81: Literary verse (Faust) beats recipe (BvgS).
    Phase 82: This is NOT because VMS is verse — it's because
    Faust has richer vocabulary (higher TTR, lower Zipf) than
    formulaic recipe text. The VOCABULARY RICHNESS dimension
    drives the closest matches, not genre or era.
    → VMS requires a plaintext model with RICH vocabulary,
    not necessarily verse or charm structure.

  OVERALL VERDICT:

    The medieval German charm hypothesis is INTERESTING but
    UNTESTABLE at current corpus size. The surviving OHG/MHG
    charm corpus (587 words) is too small for reliable
    fingerprinting. What we CAN say:

    ✓ Charm word-length distribution: measured (check P5 above)
    ✓ Charm bilingual mixing: matches f116v context
    ✗ Charm h_char: does NOT explain VMS anomaly (or NaN)
    ✗ Full fingerprint: UNRELIABLE at N=587

    The Phase 81 'Faust anomaly' is better explained by
    VOCABULARY RICHNESS than by verse/charm structure.
    The h_char anomaly (0.641) remains THE diagnostic
    signature separating VMS from ALL natural language —
    including medieval German charms.

  NEXT PHASE RECOMMENDATIONS:
    1. Test VOCABULARY RICHNESS as the key variable:
       use texts with known high/low TTR and measure
       if TTR-matched corpora cluster at similar distance.
    2. Test the ENCODING LAYER directly: apply verbose/
       positional cipher to German plaintext and check
       if h_char drops to VMS range (0.55-0.75).
    3. Source a LARGER medieval German medical/charm corpus
       (Arzneibücher, Bartholomäus, larger Segen collections)
       for a more reliable comparison.
